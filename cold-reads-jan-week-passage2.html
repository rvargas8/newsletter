Emma's Telegraph Message

Historical Fiction - Kansas, 1867

Sixteen-year-old Emma sat at her desk in the small train station. She worked as a telegraph operator. Her job was to send messages using a special machine. She tapped a key to make clicking sounds. The clicks were a code. Other telegraph operators could understand the code.

18

Emma's father had been hurt in a train accident. Now Emma worked to help her family. At first, the men at the station didn't think a girl could do the job. But Emma was fast and careful. Soon everyone trusted her.

28

One hot afternoon, a rider brought bad news. A bridge fifteen miles away had washed out in a storm. A train was coming! It would reach the broken bridge in two hours. Emma had to warn the next station quickly.

37

Her fingers flew across the telegraph key. Click-click-click. She sent the message: "BRIDGE OUT. STOP THE TRAIN."

42

Emma waited. Her heart beat fast. Then the answer came back: "MESSAGE RECEIVED. TRAIN STOPPED. THANK YOU."

48

Emma let out a big breath. She had saved the train! Mr. Peterson, her boss, came to her desk. "Good work, Miss Emma," he said. "You saved many lives today."

56

That evening, Emma walked home feeling happy. Before she learned to use the telegraph, she didn't have many choices for work. Now she had an important job. She helped keep the trains safe. Someday she might work in a big city. But today, she was glad she could help right here in Kansas.
