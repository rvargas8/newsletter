Cold Read 1: Helen Keller

Biography

Helen Keller was born in 1880 in Alabama. When she was a baby, she became very sick. After the illness, Helen could no longer see or hear. Because of this, she had trouble understanding the world around her and communicating with others.

When Helen was seven years old, a teacher named Anne Sullivan came to help her. Anne taught Helen how to communicate by spelling words into her hand. Helen learned that objects had names, and this helped her understand language. She worked hard every day and did not give up, even when learning was difficult.

As Helen grew older, she learned to read, write, and speak. She went to college and later became an author and speaker. Helen traveled around the world to talk about the importance of education and helping people with disabilities.

Helen Keller's life shows that determination and support can help people overcome challenges.

Cold Read 2: Martin Luther King Jr.

Biography

Martin Luther King Jr. was born in 1929 in Atlanta, Georgia. As a child, he noticed that some people were treated unfairly because of the color of their skin. These experiences helped shape his beliefs about fairness and equality.

As an adult, Martin Luther King Jr. became a leader in the Civil Rights Movement. He believed that change should happen peacefully. He gave powerful speeches and led marches where people protested unfair laws without using violence.

One of his most famous speeches was the "I Have a Dream" speech. In it, he shared his hope for a future where everyone would be treated equally. Because of his leadership, new laws were passed that helped make the United States more fair.

Martin Luther King Jr. is remembered for his courage, leadership, and commitment to peaceful change.

Cold Read 3: Amelia Earhart

Biography

Amelia Earhart was born in 1897. From a young age, she loved adventure and trying new things. When she saw an airplane for the first time, she became interested in flying.

At a time when very few women were pilots, Amelia worked hard to learn how to fly. She practiced often and never gave up, even when flying was dangerous. Her dedication helped her become a skilled pilot.

Amelia became famous for breaking flying records. She was the first woman to fly alone across the Atlantic Ocean. She also encouraged others, especially girls, to believe they could achieve their dreams.

Amelia Earhart is remembered as a brave woman who showed the importance of courage and determination.

Cold Read 4: Jackie Robinson

Biography

Jackie Robinson was born in 1919. He loved sports and was especially talented at baseball. Jackie worked hard to improve his skills and became an excellent player.

In 1947, Jackie Robinson made history by becoming the first Black player in Major League Baseball. At the time, many people believed baseball should only be played by white players. Jackie faced unfair treatment from fans and players.

Instead of responding with anger, Jackie stayed focused on playing his best. His strength and courage helped change baseball forever. Because of Jackie Robinson, more players of different backgrounds were given opportunities to play professional sports.

Jackie Robinson is remembered as a hero who helped bring fairness and equality to baseball.

Cold Read 5: Jane Goodall

Biography

Jane Goodall was born in 1934 and loved animals from a young age. She spent many years studying chimpanzees in Africa. Jane carefully watched how they lived, played, and worked together.

Through her research, Jane discovered that chimpanzees use tools and have emotions. This helped people understand that animals are more like humans than once believed.

Jane also worked to protect animals and their habitats. She taught others about the importance of caring for nature and respecting wildlife.

Jane Goodall is known for her dedication to science and her love for animals.
