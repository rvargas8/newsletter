The Brave Firefighter

A Non-Fiction Article

Firefighters are brave men and women who help keep our communities safe. They work in fire stations and are always ready to respond when someone calls for help. Firefighters wear special protective gear to keep them safe from heat and smoke.

12

When a fire alarm sounds, firefighters must act quickly. They put on their heavy coats, helmets, and boots in less than two minutes. Then they climb onto their fire truck and race to the emergency. The loud siren warns people to get out of the way.

25

At the scene of a fire, firefighters work as a team. Some firefighters connect hoses to fire hydrants to get water. Others use ladders to reach people who might be trapped in tall buildings. The captain gives orders to make sure everyone stays safe.

38

Firefighters use powerful hoses to spray water on fires. The water helps put out the flames by cooling them down. Sometimes firefighters also use foam or special chemicals to fight different types of fires. They must be very careful not to let the fire spread to other buildings.

52

Firefighters don't just fight fires. They also help people who are hurt in car accidents, rescue animals that are stuck in trees, and teach children about fire safety. Many firefighters visit schools to show students how to prevent fires and what to do if there is a fire.

66

To become a firefighter, people must train for many months. They learn how to use firefighting equipment, how to rescue people, and how to stay safe in dangerous situations. Firefighters must be strong, brave, and willing to help others at any time of day or night.

80

Firefighters are true heroes in our communities. They risk their own safety to help others and keep our neighborhoods safe. Next time you see a fire truck, remember that the firefighters inside are ready to help anyone who needs them.
