Lucy's Mill Work

Historical Fiction - Massachusetts, 1834

Lucy woke up when the factory bell rang at five o'clock in the morning. She was fifteen years old and worked at a big textile mill. A textile mill is a factory that makes cloth. Lucy lived in a house with other girls who also worked at the mill.

18

Inside the factory, big machines called looms made cloth all day long. The machines were very loud. Bang! Clatter! Whoosh! Lucy stood by her loom and watched it work. If a thread broke, she tied it back together. She worked for twelve hours every day except Sunday.

30

Lucy had come from her family's farm to earn money. The work was hard, but something surprised her. The mill owners had started a library! They also had talks and classes in the evenings. Lucy and the other girls could borrow books and learn new things.

42

Lucy joined a reading group with her friends. They read books together and talked about them. The girls even started their own magazine. It was called "The Lowell Offering." The mill girls wrote poems and stories for it. Lucy had written a poem about her farm, and it was going to be printed!

54

At lunch, Lucy sat with her friend Mary. They talked about the book they were reading. "Can you believe we get to read the same books that rich people read?" Mary asked with a big smile.
