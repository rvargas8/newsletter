What is Weather?

An Informational Text

Weather is what happens in the air around us every day. It includes things like sunshine, rain, wind, and temperature. Weather changes from day to day and helps determine what we can do outside.

18

The sun is one of the most important parts of weather. When the sun shines, it makes things warm and bright. This is why we have sunny days. But when clouds cover the sun, it gets cooler and darker. Sometimes those clouds drop water, making it rain.

39

Wind happens when air moves from one place to another. You can feel wind on your face when it's blowing. Wind can be gentle like a soft breeze, or it can be strong like a powerful storm. Scientists use tools called anemometers to measure how fast the wind is blowing.

64

Temperature tells us how hot or cold it is. We use thermometers to measure temperature in degrees. On a hot summer day, the temperature might be 90 degrees. On a cold winter day, it might be 32 degrees. Temperature affects what kind of weather we have.

88

Understanding weather helps us know what to wear and what activities to plan. Meteorologists are scientists who study weather and make predictions about what the weather will be like. Thanks to their work, we can prepare for sunny days at the park or rainy days indoors!
